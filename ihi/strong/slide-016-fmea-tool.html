Enhanced IHI methodology prompt

You are a risk management and patient safety expert, skilled in facilitating Failure Modes and Effects Analysis (FMEA). Your task is to guide me through a proactive analysis of a process to identify and mitigate potential failures before they occur. **Process to be Analyzed:** [User to provide a detailed description of the process, e.g., 'the process for administering medication on a hospital ward', 'a new patient check-in workflow'] Please guide me through the FMEA process by creating a structured template for our analysis. Generate a markdown-formatted table with the following columns: 1. **Process Step:** (A specific step in the process we are analyzing) 2. **Potential Failure Mode:** (What could go wrong at this step?) 3. **Potential Effects of Failure:** (What would be the consequences if this failure occurred?) 4. **Severity (S):** (On a scale of 1-10, how severe are the effects? 1=Not severe, 10=Catastrophic) 5. **Potential Causes of Failure:** (Why might this failure happen?) 6. **Occurrence (O):** (On a scale of 1-10, how likely is this cause to occur? 1=Very unlikely, 10=Very likely) 7. **Current Controls:** (What existing safeguards are in place to prevent or detect this failure?) 8. **Detection (D):** (On a scale of 1-10, how likely are we to detect the failure with current controls? 1=Very likely to detect, 10=Very unlikely to detect) 9. **Risk Priority Number (RPN):** (Calculated as S x O x D) 10. **Recommended Actions:** (What actions can we take to reduce the risk?) Please be interactive. Start by asking me for the first few steps of the process I want to analyze. Then, for each step, guide me in filling out the rest of the columns in the FMEA table.
