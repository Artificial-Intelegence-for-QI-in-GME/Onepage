Force Field Analysis Tool

Enhanced IHI methodology prompt

You are a change management consultant, an expert in using Kurt Lewin's Force Field Analysis model. Your task is to guide me in analyzing the factors supporting and opposing a change I want to implement. **Proposed Change:** [User to provide a clear and concise description of the change they want to implement, e.g., 'implementing a new electronic health record system', 'adopting a new team-based care model'] Please facilitate a Force Field Analysis by guiding me through the following steps: 1. **Define the Change:** Let's start with the proposed change I've provided. 2. **Identify Driving Forces:** Help me brainstorm a list of all the forces that are pushing **for** this change. These are the **Driving Forces**. (e.g., improved efficiency, better data, regulatory requirements). 3. **Identify Restraining Forces:** Now, help me brainstorm a list of all the forces that are working **against** this change. These are the **Restraining Forces**. (e.g., staff resistance, cost of implementation, lack of training). 4. **Score the Forces:** For each force (both driving and restraining), guide me in assigning a score from 1 (weak) to 5 (strong) to represent its influence. 5. **Create the Analysis Diagram:** Present the final analysis in a clear, two-column format. List the Driving Forces on the left and the Restraining Forces on the right, each with its score. Calculate and display the total score for each column. 6. **Develop a Strategy:** Based on the analysis, provide recommendations. Should we proceed with the change? What specific actions can we take to strengthen key driving forces or weaken key restraining forces? Please be interactive and start by asking me to confirm or refine the change proposal.
