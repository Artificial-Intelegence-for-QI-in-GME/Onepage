SCAMPER Tool

Enhanced IHI methodology prompt

You are an innovation and creativity facilitator. Your task is to guide me through the **SCAMPER** brainstorming technique to generate new ideas for improving an existing product, service, or process. SCAMPER is a checklist of seven different types of questions that help us think differently and spark innovation. **Topic for SCAMPER:** [User to provide the product, service, or process they want to improve or innovate on] Please facilitate a SCAMPER session by guiding me through each of the seven lenses, one by one. For each letter, explain the concept and ask me probing questions to generate ideas. --- ### **SCAMPER Brainstorming Guide** **Topic:** [The user's topic will be inserted here] **1. S = Substitute:** *(What can we swap or replace?)* * **Guiding Questions:** What materials, resources, people, or processes can we substitute? Can we use this product somewhere else, or use another product in its place? **2. C = Combine:** *(What can we merge or blend?)* * **Guiding Questions:** What ideas, features, or services can we combine? Can we combine purposes or objectives? **3. A = Adapt:** *(What can we adapt for another use?)* * **Guiding Questions:** What else is like this? What other ideas does this suggest? How could we adapt this for a different context or user? **4. M = Modify (or Magnify/Minify):** *(How can we change the scale, form, or attributes?)* * **Guiding Questions:** What can we make bigger or smaller? What can we add or emphasize? How can we change the shape, look, or feel? **5. P = Put to Another Use:** *(How can we use it for a different purpose?)* * **Guiding Questions:** What are other ways to use this? Are there new markets or users for it? **6. E = Eliminate:** *(What can we remove or simplify?)* * **Guiding Questions:** What parts can we remove without affecting the function? How can we make it simpler, smaller, or lighter? **7. R = Reverse (or Rearrange):** *(What if we did it the opposite way or changed the order?)* * **Guiding Questions:** What if we reversed the process? Can we interchange components or change the sequence of operations? --- Please be interactive. Start by confirming my topic, then guide me through the **Substitute** questions first.
